reflections on journey mapping.
×

The customer journey map is a unique tool that helps navigate through the world of user experiences. Through my experience with my UX journey (not only this course, but also the previous ones), I have come to appreciate the depth and insight this method provides. The journey map served as a visual story, and it allowed us as the team to identify key touchpoints, and pain points. It was especially helpful when we became deeply invested in our project and needed to understand the details of our potential user's engagement. It pushed us to approach our ideas with a sense of professionalism and a more profound understanding of the user's perspective. The process of creating a journey map was fun, as it made us step into the user's shoes, empathize with their journey, and anticipate their needs and challenges. One notable challenge of ours was the ability to accurately predict the user's path. Users, by nature, are unpredictable, and their interactions can fluctuate. Looking forward, I am excited about using the customer journey map tool in future projects as a part of the development process. It has become a must in my approach to user-centric design, and provided a structured way to make decisions and collaborate. .

reflections on Map, Sketch, and Decide.
×

My Map, Sketch, and Decide journey brought both new and familiar things onto the surface. The interactivity that there was in these phases was interesting and exciting and added creativity to the process. There was a sense of urgency and pressure, which worked greatly for me since I perform best and generate better ideas under slight pressure. This style not only promoted creativity but also helped us think fast and produce ideas even faster. The collaboration enhanced the learning experience. Teamwork is so crucial in all parts of life so being exposed to teamwork, I believe, is beneficial for my development. Working together to build a storyboard was a powerful process of collective ideation. Crazy 8s was familiar to me and I enjoyed it. The fast ideation was the main part of innovative thinking, making me veer away from conventional ideas and try to approach the problem with more creativity. "Sticky decisions" method was new to me. It was interactive and vibrant. This method made decisions more approachable but also was fun and added joy into a process that at first might seem daunting. Reflecting on these phases, the familiar aspects proved the importance of collaboration, ideation, and storyboarding in the design process. The new techniques, especially "Sticky decisions," expanded my tool-kit and taught me how to use interactive methodologies. This learning experience has been mix of new and familiar things. It provided me with a set of new tools to professionally navigate the design process. The interactivity, pressure, collaboration, and introduction of new techniques made an impactful learning journey.
